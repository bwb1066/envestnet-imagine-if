The situation

On the morning of Tuesday Oct 14, three signals converged that turned "when we can" into "this week." Sage saw them within minutes of each other and connected them.

SAGE — three signals, one window · detected 8:47am Tuesday

Signal 1 — direct-indexing harvest log. The Walker direct-indexing sleeve had accumulated $94K of realized losses YTD, waiting to be paired against gains. That number had crossed the $80K threshold I track for "now becomes valuable."

Signal 3 — Carolyn's mention. In the Oct 9 meeting Sarah captured to the CRM: "Carolyn asked about whether we 'still have all that company stock from Greg's old job.' She seemed uncomfortable about the concentration." Tagged it sentiment-flagged. I weighted that the highest.

SAGE — why this is the case

This intervention was not magic. An experienced advisor with infinite time would have done the same thing. The point is that Sarah's time isn't infinite. She has 350 households. The concentrated-position-with-harvest-window opportunity at the Walker household competed with 12 other "important but not urgent" items on Sarah's mental to-do list — and concentration windows close fast.

What Sage did was three things humans struggle to do at scale: watched the harvest log against the position list continuously, connected an earnings-driven price move to a wash-sale window clearing, and weighted Carolyn's casual sentiment cue from a meeting 5 days prior into the priority calculation. None of that requires sophistication. It requires attention across 350 households simultaneously, which is what platforms are for.

And: Sarah made the decision. Sarah ran the meeting. Sarah approved the trades. Sarah sent the follow-up. Sage carried the cognitive load between her decisions, not the decisions themselves. Total Sarah-time across the 4-day workflow: 3 hours 23 minutes. The manual baseline would have been a 16-week passive drift before the next quarterly review window — at which point the price would have moved, the harvest pool would have decayed, and Carolyn's casual question might not have been raised again. The $186K wouldn't have shown up as a missed opportunity. It would have shown up as the absence of an opportunity that was never recognized.
