How Sage orchestrated this

1

Knowledge capture

Bob recorded 14 hours of "relationship reflections" over 6 weeks last year. Sage transcribed, structured, and indexed by household. Every receiving advisor gets Bob's voice on every transferred household.

2

Fit scoring

Sage scored every Bob-household × receiving-advisor pairing on six dimensions. Pairings >75% suggested directly; 50–75% surfaced as judgment calls for the partners.

3

Intro sequencing

For each transferred household: Bob email → joint Zoom intro → Sage-drafted personalized welcome from new advisor referencing Bob's specific relationship notes → first scheduled review within 90 days.

4

Retention tracking

Sage monitors every transferred household for retention signals: response rates, meeting completion, portfolio-engagement, sentiment in comms. 98% retention to date vs 87% industry baseline for retirement-driven transitions.

SAGE — what's protecting the 98% retention

Bob's voice in every intro. The first thing the receiving advisor says references something specific to that household — the daughter's name, the trip to Greece, the dog. None of that is in the CRM. It's in Bob's recorded reflections that Sage transcribed and indexed. The household feels recognized, not handed off.

Sage's continuity model. When a Bob-household transfers to Sarah, Sage carries the relationship's narrative forward inside the firm's CRM — meeting notes Bob made in 2019 surface in 2026 when relevant. The household experiences continuity even though the advisor has changed.

The 2% that didn't retain. 6 households moved their assets — 4 to family-member-led firms (out of our control), 2 to competitors. Sage flagged 5 of the 6 as elevated-risk at the time of intro; 4 of the 5 left within 90 days. The retention model is calibrating; next-wave intros will weight risk signals harder.
