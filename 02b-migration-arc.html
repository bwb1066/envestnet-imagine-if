SAGE — the three decisions this trajectory hides

2029 is the inflection year. Greg's earnout completes when he's 57 — too young to retire by most standard frames, but the household won't need additional income. The decision he'll face is what to do with his time, not his money. The financial plan adapts to whichever path he picks; the agenda for the 2029 annual review is the path conversation itself.

The Marcus conversation around 2031. Carolyn's framing was "Marcus is settled, doesn't need our help." That may stay true. But teacher salaries don't grow the way attending-physician salaries do, and Bri's wealth trajectory will diverge sharply once she's an attending. The gap can create family dynamics. Worth a soft conversation by 2031, before Bri's compensation step-changes.

Carolyn's retirement. She's never said she wants to retire — she clearly loves the dean role. But the household won't need her income, and the CCC pension calculation is meaningfully better if she works to 62. Worth surfacing in 2032 so she has the data, not because she has to decide.
