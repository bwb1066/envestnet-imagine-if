SAGE — the firm's productivity story this quarter

Time recaptured: 480 hours across the 6 advisors in Q3, mostly in compliance documentation auto-population, rebalance ticket prep, and client-comm drafting. That's roughly 80 hours per advisor — a workweek and a half each.

Model adoption is the biggest leverage shift. A year ago, 35% of firm AUM was in custom-built portfolios per advisor. Now 94% is in PMC-approved models with documented overrides for the remaining 6%. This is the single biggest factor in cleaner attestation and cleaner audit prep — and it's been Sage nudging advisors toward model-aligned positions over many small decisions.

Two governance flags before Q4 closes: the 6 model-drift households exceeding the 14-day grace window need batch action this week, and the 23 overdue annual reviews need scheduling. Both have auto-drafted outreach awaiting partner approval.
